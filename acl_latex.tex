\begin{abstract}
Ensuring large language model (LLM) reliability requires \textit{solvability boundary learning}: distinguishing query-inherent defects that block a determinate answer from subjective capability limits on otherwise feasible tasks. Current LLMs often conflate these dimensions, hallucinating on broken queries or over-refusing valid but difficult problems. We introduce \textbf{UnsolvableQA}, a verifier-backed multi-domain benchmark of logically flawed queries constructed via Reverse Construction with deterministic checks and audited math verification, and \textbf{UnsolvableRL}, an alignment framework that decouples three behaviors: reasoning on solvable tasks, detecting unsolvability, and capability-calibrated refusal. UnsolvableRL yields over 85\% unsolvability detection on Qwen3-4B while improving solvable accuracy from 43.4\% to 69.4\%, with a +35.4-point solvable-accuracy gain on Llama3-8B. Beyond binary detection, diagnostic tuning distinguishes missing-information defects from constraint conflicts and transfers zero-shot to clarification on IN3, improving Detail Recovery from 47.8\% to 60.7\% for Llama3-8B. Finally, we identify Capability Collapse: refusal penalties degrade detection when applied without unsolvable data, but act as a rigor regularizer when such data are included.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

As Large Language Models (LLMs) achieve strong performance on complex reasoning tasks~\citep{chen2025towards,wei2022chain,guo2025deepseek,jaech2024openai,ICLR2024_d53538ba}, a key research direction \citep{qin2025largelanguagemodelsmeet} is ensuring they recognize their own limitations and behave reliably. Prior work on confidence calibration~\citep{xiong2023can,li2024confidence,yoon2025reasoning,fisch2022calibrated} and capability boundaries~\citep{NEURIPS2024_62ab1c2c,zhang2025self,mei2025reasoning,kalai2025languagemodelshallucinate,chen2025aware} examines when models should abstain from unproductive reasoning; TruthRL~\citep{wei2025truthrl} further encourages refusal under uncertainty via a ternary reward.

However, not all failures arise because a task is merely too difficult for the model. A key limitation of existing approaches is that they mainly focus on refusing questions that are \textit{solvable but currently beyond the model's capabilities} (Figure~\ref{fig:intro_quadrant}(a)). This emphasis overlooks a fundamental challenge: \textit{objectively unsolvable} queries with logical contradictions or missing information that prevents a determinate answer under the requested response format. We use \textit{objective unsolvability} in a practical sense, referring to input-side flaws that block a determinate answer under the task specification, rather than formal undecidability in computability theory. Current LLMs often conflate objective unsolvability with subjective task difficulty. As a result, they face a ``refusal dilemma'': they may hallucinate confident answers to invalid queries, or over-refuse valid but difficult problems.

We therefore treat \textit{Solution Space} (solvable vs. unsolvable) and \textit{Question Difficulty} (easy vs. hard) as orthogonal dimensions (Figure~\ref{fig:intro_quadrant}(b)). Conflating ``the problem is broken'' with ``I am unable to solve it'' introduces reward ambiguity in RL; a reliable system should separate three behaviors: (1) solving feasible tasks with rigorous reasoning; (2) detecting input-side defects and marking them unsolvable (yellow area); and (3) calibrating refusal when a solvable problem exceeds current capacity.

We introduce \textbf{UnsolvableQA}, a benchmark suite of paired solvable and unsolvable instances across logic and mathematics, with science-domain diagnostic and OOD extensions, built via \textit{Reverse Construction} with constraint conflicts and missing-information cases for diagnostic analysis. Verifier-backed structured domains use deterministic solvers; math instances undergo cross-model verification and human auditing. We further propose \textbf{UnsolvableRL} (GRPO~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath}), optimizing (1) unsolvability detection, (2) a false-unsolvability penalty, and (3) dynamic capability calibration when empirical accuracy falls below a target threshold.

Empirical results show solvability boundary learning acts as an effective reasoning regularizer. UnsolvableRL improves the unsolvable detection rate of Qwen3-4B from 38.8\% to 87.5\%, while simultaneously increasing solvable problem accuracy from 43.4\% to 69.4\%. On Llama3-8B, mean score improves from 25.2\% to 55.1\% (+29.9pp; 119\% relative improvement), with OOD gains on GPQA (+12.0\%) and MMLU-Pro (+4.5\%). Diagnostic tuning enables MI/CC attribution and IN3 clarification transfer (Detail Recovery 47.8\%$\rightarrow$60.7\% for Llama3-8B). Ablations reveal \textit{Capability Collapse} when penalties are applied without unsolvable data, whereas our joint framework turns them into a rigor regularizer.

Overall, our main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=16pt, itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt]
    \item We propose \textit{solvability boundary learning}, formally decoupling query-inherent defects from subjective capability limits, and show that treating them as orthogonal axes is algorithmically essential to resolve reward ambiguity and prevent capability collapse in RLVR.
    \item We construct \textbf{UnsolvableQA}, a verifier-backed benchmark suite spanning logic and math, with science-domain diagnostic/OOD evaluation, built via Reverse Construction with deterministic checks and audited math verification.
    \item We propose \textbf{UnsolvableRL}, decoupling reasoning on solvable tasks, unsolvability detection, and capability-calibrated refusal via $R_{\text{acc}} + R_{\text{detect}} + R_{\text{cal}}$.
    \item We show that solvability-boundary learning acts as a general reasoning regularizer with Pareto and OOD gains, and extend beyond binary detection to two-family defect diagnosis (missing information and constraint conflict) with zero-shot clarification transfer on IN3.
\end{itemize}
